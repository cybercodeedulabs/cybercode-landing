Introduction to Networking (CCNA)

Course Overview: This course introduces the foundational concepts of computer networking aligned with the Cisco CCNA certification path. Gain practical knowledge of network devices, protocols, subnetting, and troubleshooting.

Who Should Take This Course?

  • Students and beginners in IT and networking
  • Engineers planning to pursue CCNA certification
  • System administrators seeking networking fundamentals
  • Anyone preparing for technical interviews in networking

Course Benefits

  • Hands-on labs with Packet Tracer and GNS3
  • Clear understanding of OSI and TCP/IP models
  • Preparation for CCNA 200-301 certification topics
  • Exposure to network commands and troubleshooting

Topics Covered

  • Networking Basics and Topologies
  • OSI & TCP/IP Models
  • IP Addressing and Subnetting
  • Switching and Routing Fundamentals
  • VLANs and Inter-VLAN Routing
  • Network Security Basics
  • Static and Dynamic Routing (RIP, OSPF)

Tools and Platforms You'll Learn

  • Cisco Packet Tracer
  • GNS3 (Graphical Network Simulator)
  • Wireshark (packet analysis)
  • Command-line interface (CLI)

Real-World Applications

With networking as the backbone of all modern IT systems, this course equips you with the skills to configure, troubleshoot, and maintain network environments across enterprise infrastructures and cloud deployments.
